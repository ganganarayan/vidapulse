Questions

Does VidaPulse host videos?

No. VidaPulse does not host your video. You keep hosting it wherever it already lives, and VidaPulse adds only the analytics layer on top through an embed. You paste your existing video URL, VidaPulse wraps it in an analytics player, and you drop one line of script or a script-free iframe onto your page. Nothing is uploaded, moved, or re-hosted, and your video keeps its current URL.

What VidaPulse does instead of hosting

VidaPulse is an analytics layer, not a video host. The split is clean: your host stores and delivers the video file; VidaPulse measures how people watch it. You paste a URL from a supported source, such as YouTube, Amazon S3, Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, Azure Blob, Loom, a Zoom recording, Vimeo, or a direct MP4 or HLS file, and VidaPulse wraps that URL in an analytics player.

To put it on a page, you embed a single line of script or a script-free iframe. It works on WordPress, Webflow, ClickFunnels, Squarespace, or plain HTML, with no coding required. The video continues streaming from its original home; VidaPulse simply records playback behavior around it.

Why not re-hosting is an advantage

Because VidaPulse never takes custody of your file, you avoid the costs and risks that come with moving video to a new platform. The benefits are concrete:

  • No migration. You do not re-upload large files or change embeds across your pages. The video you have today is the video you measure today.
  • No extra storage or bandwidth cost. Your existing host keeps serving the file, so you are not paying a second platform to store and stream it again.
  • No lock-in. Your video never lives inside VidaPulse, so there is nothing to extract if you stop. You keep full control of where the file sits.
  • Same URL, same delivery. Viewers stream from the same place at the same quality; only the analytics are added.

This is the core design choice behind VidaPulse: separate the analytics from the hosting so you get retention data without the disruption of changing where your video lives.
