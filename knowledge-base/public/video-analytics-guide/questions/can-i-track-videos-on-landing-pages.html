Can I track videos on landing pages?

Yes, and this is exactly what VidaPulse is built for. You paste your video URL, VidaPulse wraps it in an analytics player, and you embed it on your landing page with one line of script or a script-free iframe. It works on any page builder, including WordPress, Webflow, ClickFunnels, and Squarespace, as well as custom HTML, with no coding required. You then get retention, source attribution, and conversion tracking for the specific video on that page.

Tracking a video on any landing page

A landing page usually has one job, and a video is often the thing doing it. VidaPulse measures that video where it sells. You paste the URL of a video you already host (YouTube, Amazon S3, Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, Azure Blob, Loom, a Zoom recording, Vimeo, or a direct MP4 or HLS file), VidaPulse wraps it in an analytics player, and you place it on the page.

Embedding is deliberately simple, with two options that fit any setup:

  • One-line script — drop a single line into the page and the analytics player renders in place.
  • Script-free iframe — for builders that restrict scripts, paste an iframe instead.

Both work on WordPress, Webflow, ClickFunnels, Squarespace, and plain HTML, so the page builder you already use is almost certainly supported. There is no re-hosting; the video keeps streaming from its current home.

What you learn about the video on that page

Because VidaPulse measures the video where it actually lives in your funnel, the data reflects the real audience landing on that page, not generic platform traffic. For the video on the page you get:

  • Retention — the audience-retention curve, average watch time, play rate, and the percentage of viewers reaching any point, including your offer.
  • Source — UTM and source attribution showing which campaign or channel sent each viewer, so you can compare how sources behave on that page.
  • Conversions — on Pro, conversion and CTA tracking connects watching the video to the action that follows on the page.
  • Depth on Pro — second-by-second heatmaps and viewer-level history, with no personal data collected (first-party UUID).

Together these turn the video on your landing page into a funnel you can read: how many people watched, how far they got, where they came from, and whether watching led to the action you wanted.

Why this is the core use case

VidaPulse is made for people who use video to sell on a page: coaches running VSLs, B2B teams sending demo videos, and product marketers driving sign-ups. The whole design, paste a URL, wrap it, embed one line, assumes the video lives on a landing page you control rather than on a video platform's watch page.

That is why landing-page tracking is not an edge case but the main path. You measure the same video, on the same page, against the same traffic you are already paying for, so the retention and conversion data point straight at what to fix to get more viewers to the offer.
